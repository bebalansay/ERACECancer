Research Opportunities for Students

Interested in getting involved with research? Check out these Stanford faculty profiles:

Bang Nguyen, MPP, DrPH:

  • Affiliations: Stanford Cancer Institute (Present), Cancer Prevention Institute of California (CPIC) (2003 - 2006)
  • Research Interests:

      • Cancer Prevention and Early Detection.  Dr. Nguyen is interested in prevention and early detection of breast, cervical, colorectal, and liver cancer. He is a principal investigator on a study to evaluate the effectiveness of a lay health worker intervention supported by a media campaign to increase colorectal cancer screening in Vietnamese Americans.

  • Contact Dr. Nguyen

Ingrid Oakley-Girvan, PhD, MPH:

  • Affiliations: Stanford University Department of Health Research and Policy, Cancer Prevention Institute of California
  • Research Interests:

      • Gene-environment interactions as they relate to cancer onset and progression. Dr. Oakley-Girvan uses data generated from genetic and molecular assays to explore disparities in breast, prostate, thyroid and other cancers.

  • Contact Dr. Oakley-Girvan

Marilyn Winkleby, PhD, MPH:

  • Affiliations: Faculty Director of the Stanford Office of Community Health, Faculty at the Stanford Prevention Research Center
  • Research Interests:

      • Dr. Winkleby studies the health status of ethnic minority and low socioeconomic groups with a specific focus on community-based intervention studies. Dr. Winkleby also founded the 22-year-old Stanford Medical Youth Science Program, a summer residential pre-college pipeline program that is dedicated to encouraging low-income high school students to pursue higher education to prepare them for medical and other scientific careers

  • Contact Dr. Winkleby
